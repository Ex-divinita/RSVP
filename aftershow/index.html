TIRANA INTERNATIONAL FAIR

30th AfterShow Report - Interactive Edition

UNITE, INVEST, INNOVATE | 6-9 NOVEMBER 2024

1. Highlights

Exhibition Space: 6,850 m2
Total Visitors & Attendees: 21,285
Exhibitors: 270
Countries Represented: 15 (18 including delegations)
Concurrent Events: 64
B2B/B2C/B2G Meetings: More than 1,200 companies

2. Introduction

We are proud to present the aftershow report for the 30th anniversary edition of the Tirana International Fair, a milestone event celebrating three decades of fostering economic growth, innovation, and regional cooperation. This special edition featured the **30for30's Pavilion**, highlighting Albania's leading companies that have been operational for over 30 years.

The fair welcomed participation from 18 countries, reinforcing its role as a hub for international collaboration and commerce. Italy and Serbia stood out with the largest participation among foreign countries, contributing 35 and 25 companies, respectively. With 270 exhibitors and 21,285 attendees, this year's edition exemplified the Tirana International Fair's legacy as a bridge connecting Albania to the world.

3. Opening Ceremony Remarks

Remarks from Minister Blendi Gonxhja

+
Minister of Economy, Culture and Innovation, hailed the fair as a cornerstone of Albania's regional economic integration. Reflecting on Klik Ekspo Group's journey, he remarked: "When I first met Luan at the National Historical Museum, he was a man brimming with energy and vision. Thirty years later, Klik Ekspo Group has grown into more than just a private company-it has become a public treasure, a bridge connecting Albanian entrepreneurs with the global market. Today, this platform embodies Albania's aspirations for innovation and international cooperation."
- Mr. Blendi Gonxhja

Vice Mayor of Tirana - Mr. Andi Seferi

Deputy Mayor of Tirana, lauded the fair's transformative role in the city's evolution, saying: "This fair is now an integral part of Tirana's 104-year history, representing nearly a third of the city's lifespan as the capital. Klik Ekspo Group has given Tirana a gift that keeps on giving -a platform for investors and entrepreneurs that has beautified the city and strengthened its economic foundation."

5. Key State Participants

Italy

Italy emerged as the largest international participant with 35 companies, showcasing products from Sicilian Arance Rosse to cutting-edge green transport. Remains Albania's top trading partner (49% of exports).

Serbia

Second-largest international participant with 25 companies, highlighting expertise in construction, manufacturing, and food processing. Strong bilateral ties fostered over 20 consecutive years.

7. 30for30's Pavilion

This pavilion celebrated the enduring success and resilience of companies marking 30 years in the Albanian market, including Bolv Group, Lim Em Company, Aiba Company, Tirana Bank, Agna Group, and Coca-Cola Bottling Shqiperia.

Coca-Cola Bottling Shqiperia Focus

+

Expanded Detail: Sustainability & Innovation. Coca-Cola Bottling Shqiperia, a symbol of global brands in the local market, presented its local impact in terms of employment, distribution networks, and sustainability initiatives. The company emphasized how it has maintained its leadership position through innovation and customer engagement, specifically highlighting modernized production facilities and the implementation of sustainable practices in line with global environmental standards, including their eco-friendly fleet.

8. Conferences and Panel Discussions

  • Regional Cooperation Roundtable: Focused on cross-border trade, regional infrastructure, and economic collaboration in the Western Balkans.
  • The Banking Sector in Albania: Addressed digital transformation, transparency, and accountability in the evolving banking landscape.
  • Competitiveness and Cohesion: Organized by the Hungarian Embassy, this session highlighted Hungary's EU Presidency focus on regional partnerships.
  • Albania's Strategic Investment Opportunities: AIC highlighted projects like Durana Tech Park and Expo Albania.
  • Consume and Grow Locally: Discussed strategies to boost local production and transition toward international exports.
